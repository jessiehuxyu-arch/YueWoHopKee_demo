第一章 · Chapter IChapter I

西營盤的
老字號根基Roots in
Sai Ying Pun

裕和合記由東莞人方蔭芬於一九七三年創立，紮根西營盤德輔道西，迄今逾五十年。早年由方氏家族親力打理，其後由子方沛源及女婿傅秋榮接手，三代相傳，延續這份港式臘味的獨特風味。

Founded in 1973 by Fong Yam-fan from Dongguan, Yu Wo Hop Kee has been rooted at Des Voeux Road West, Sai Ying Pun for over fifty years. From the founding family to son Fong Pui-yuen and son-in-law Fu Chau-wing, the shop has passed through three generations of stewardship.

店舖樓底甚高，臘腸靠牆向上多層排疊，掛滿整面牆壁，連成一片「腸棚」；部分臘腸懸空掛於店中高處，不論環顧還是仰視，各式臘味盡入眼簾，是西營盤街道上最引人注目的風景之一。

The shop's high ceilings allow sausages to be stacked in layers against the walls, forming a spectacular curtain of preserved meats. Whether you look sideways or upward, the sight of glistening lap cheong cascading from the rafters is one of Sai Ying Pun's most iconic street scenes.

「裕和畀咗我好多，我嘅家庭、仔女一切都係佢畀我。」
"Yu Wo has given me so much — my family, my children, everything."
— 傅秋榮師傅 · Master Fu Chau-wing
第二章 · Chapter IIChapter II

荃灣工場
人手製作Handcrafted
in Tsuen Wan

裕和於荃灣橫龍街自設工場，每年八月開臘，師傅們清晨五時已到崗，開始繁複的製作工序。以機器將梅頭瘦肉與豬脾肉切丁，再以鹽、糖、豉油及玫瑰露醃製調味，人手拌勻後灌入豬粉腸腸衣。

Yu Wo operates its own factory on Heng Lung Street, Tsuen Wan. From August each year, craftsmen begin at 5am, cutting shoulder and spleen pork into cubes, seasoning with salt, sugar, soy sauce and rose wine, then hand-mixing before stuffing into pork casings.

灌腸後需人手打針窿疏氣排水，再綁水草、扎繩結，扛起竹枝入焙爐以低溫烘焗，最後靠師傅每天人手轉兩次竹，確保臘腸受熱均勻。整個製作過程約需四天，方能製成逾千斤色澤油亮、香氣四溢的臘腸膶腸。

After stuffing, craftsmen hand-prick each sausage to release air and moisture, then tie with rushes. The sausages are loaded onto bamboo racks and kiln-dried at low heat, with workers manually rotating every rack twice daily for even drying. The full process takes four days to produce over a thousand catties of gleaming, fragrant sausage.

第三章 · Chapter IIIChapter III

第三代接棒
傳承與創新Third Generation:
Tradition Meets Innovation

傅志聰（Sam）身為第三代，原從事銀行業，後毅然放棄穩定工作，回歸家業。Sam坦言起初以為幫忙不難，跟老師傅們學習後方知製作臘腸「成日都要擔擔抬抬，濕手濕腳」，遠比想像辛苦。

Third-generation Sam Fu left a stable banking career to join the family business. He candidly admits he initially underestimated the work — after learning from the master craftsmen, he discovered that sausage-making involves constant heavy lifting and wet hands, far more demanding than he'd imagined.

Sam逐漸為自己找到定位，主力宣傳推廣，開設Facebook及Instagram帳戶，定期分享產品知識，引導公眾認識臘味文化。與老師傅合作研發低糖臘腸及黑松露臘腸皇等創新產品，令老字號煥發新生。

Sam found his role in marketing and innovation — launching the shop's Facebook and Instagram, and collaborating with master craftsmen to develop new products like low-sugar sausages and the Black Truffle Supreme, breathing fresh life into this half-century institution.

「若果有朝一日，香港沒有了本地製造的臘腸，會很可惜。」
"If the day comes when Hong Kong no longer makes its own lap cheong, that would be a great loss."
— 傅志聰（Sam）· Third Generation
